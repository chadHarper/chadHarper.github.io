Current Roles

Ph.D. candidate in Physics at the University of California, Berkeley, and graduate fellow at the Kavli Center for Ethics, Science, and the Public.

Research

I study how local structure shapes global behavior in complex networked systems, using tools from statistical physics, information theory, and dynamical systems.

Teaching

I teach physics at Laney College and mathematics at Mount Tamalpais College at San Quentin, including a no-prerequisite course on the physics of complex systems.

Prior Industry Work

Before returning to graduate school, I worked for three years as a Scientific Data Analyst and Consultant at Deloitte, building machine learning pipelines and graph-based models for government clients.

Writing and Creative Practice

Alongside my scientific work, I write about fashion, aesthetics, and culture, and I direct editorial-style photoshoots.
